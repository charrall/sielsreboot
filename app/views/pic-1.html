Do you have a prior import consent document?

You will usually need a prior import consent document to export civil firearm products. If you do not provide one, this may delay processing of your case or result in your licence application being refused.

Help with prior import consent

Your application may require a prior import consent (PIC) document to show that the destination country is content to import the firearms.

However, if a country does not class a certain product as a firearm, a PIC document may not be issued, and an end-user undertaking would be sufficient instead. It must be submitted together with a letter or email from the relevant importing country's licensing authority stating that a PIC document or equivalent cannot be issued. You can use the 'Additional information' section to upload any supporting documents.
